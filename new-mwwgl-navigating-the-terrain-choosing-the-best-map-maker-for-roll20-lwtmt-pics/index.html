Navigating the Terrain: Choosing the Best Map Maker for Roll20

Roll20, a popular virtual tabletop platform, empowers game masters and players to create and experience immersive roleplaying adventures. However, the platform’s success hinges on the ability to visually represent the game world, and this is where map makers play a crucial role. While Roll20 offers its own built-in map editor, many users turn to external tools for greater flexibility, control, and artistic expression.

This article explores the landscape of map makers designed for Roll20, examining their strengths, weaknesses, and suitability for various gaming needs. By understanding the nuances of each tool, game masters can select the best map maker for their specific requirements, ultimately enhancing the visual appeal and immersion of their virtual campaigns.

A Comparative Overview of Popular Map Makers for Roll20

1. Inkarnate

Inkarnate stands out as a user-friendly, web-based map maker, offering a vast library of pre-made assets and intuitive tools for creating detailed maps. Its strengths lie in its accessibility, particularly for beginners. The platform’s drag-and-drop interface allows users to quickly assemble maps using pre-designed elements, such as forests, mountains, and buildings.

Inkarnate’s strengths:

  • Ease of Use: The intuitive interface and pre-made assets make map creation accessible even to novice users.
  • Vast Asset Library: A diverse collection of pre-designed assets allows for rapid map creation and customization.
  • Web-Based Platform: Accessible from any device with an internet connection, eliminating the need for software installations.
  • Community Support: A vibrant online community provides resources and inspiration for map making.

Inkarnate’s limitations:

  • Limited Customization: While offering a wide range of assets, the customization options for individual elements might be limited for users seeking highly detailed maps.
  • Subscription Model: A subscription is required for full access to all features and assets.

2. Dungeon Painter Studio

Dungeon Painter Studio, a desktop application, is a powerful tool for creating high-quality maps with intricate details. It offers a comprehensive set of tools for drawing, painting, and manipulating assets, allowing for a high degree of customization.

Dungeon Painter Studio’s strengths:

  • Advanced Customization: Provides extensive tools for detailed drawing, painting, and asset manipulation.
  • Powerful Features: Offers advanced features like layering, perspective, and custom brushes, catering to experienced map makers.
  • Offline Access: Allows for map creation without an internet connection.
  • Importing and Exporting: Supports a wide range of image formats for importing and exporting maps.

Dungeon Painter Studio’s limitations:

  • Steeper Learning Curve: The comprehensive toolset and advanced features require a steeper learning curve compared to web-based options.
  • Desktop Application: Requires installation on a computer, limiting access to users without appropriate hardware.

3. Wonderdraft

Wonderdraft, a desktop application, focuses on creating maps with a focus on worldbuilding and strategic design. It offers tools for generating landscapes, creating political boundaries, and placing settlements, making it ideal for crafting expansive campaign maps.

Wonderdraft’s strengths:

  • Worldbuilding Focus: Provides tools for creating large-scale maps with a focus on worldbuilding and strategic design.
  • Procedural Generation: Features tools for generating landscapes and terrain, allowing for quick and efficient map creation.
  • Customizable Style: Offers a range of customizable styles and assets for creating unique and visually appealing maps.
  • Exporting Options: Allows for exporting maps in various formats, including Roll20-compatible files.

Wonderdraft’s limitations:

  • Limited Detail: While suitable for large-scale maps, it might not be ideal for creating detailed maps with intricate features.
  • Desktop Application: Requires installation on a computer, limiting access to users without appropriate hardware.

4. Cartographer

Cartographer, a web-based map maker, distinguishes itself by its focus on creating highly detailed maps. It offers an extensive set of tools for drawing, painting, and manipulating assets, allowing for a level of precision comparable to desktop applications.

Cartographer’s strengths:

  • Detailed Map Creation: Provides tools for creating highly detailed maps with intricate features.
  • Customizable Assets: Offers a wide range of customizable assets and tools for creating unique and visually appealing maps.
  • Web-Based Platform: Accessible from any device with an internet connection, eliminating the need for software installations.
  • Community Support: A vibrant online community provides resources and inspiration for map making.

Cartographer’s limitations:

  • Learning Curve: The extensive toolset and features require a steeper learning curve compared to simpler web-based options.
  • Subscription Model: A subscription is required for full access to all features and assets.

5. MapTool

MapTool, a free and open-source desktop application, offers a comprehensive suite of tools for creating and managing maps, including features for online play. Its strengths lie in its flexibility and its ability to integrate seamlessly with Roll20.

MapTool’s strengths:

  • Free and Open Source: No cost barrier to entry, allowing users to access all features without a subscription.
  • Comprehensive Toolset: Offers a wide range of tools for creating maps, managing tokens, and integrating with Roll20.
  • Online Play Support: Includes features for online play, including turn-based combat and integrated dice rolling.
  • Community Support: A large and active community provides resources and support for users.

MapTool’s limitations:

  • Steeper Learning Curve: The comprehensive toolset and advanced features require a steeper learning curve compared to simpler options.
  • Desktop Application: Requires installation on a computer, limiting access to users without appropriate hardware.

Choosing the Right Map Maker for Your Needs

The best map maker for Roll20 depends on the individual’s needs and preferences. Consider the following factors when making a selection:

  • Ease of Use: For beginners or users seeking a quick and intuitive map creation experience, web-based options like Inkarnate offer a smooth learning curve.
  • Customization: For those who require extensive customization options and detailed maps, desktop applications like Dungeon Painter Studio and Cartographer provide greater control.
  • Worldbuilding Focus: For creating expansive campaign maps with a focus on worldbuilding, Wonderdraft offers tools for generating landscapes and strategic design.
  • Budget: Free and open-source options like MapTool are excellent choices for budget-conscious users, while subscription-based services offer access to a wider range of features and assets.
  • Online Play Integration: For online play, MapTool offers a seamless integration with Roll20, while other tools might require additional steps to import maps.

FAQs

Q: Can I use free map makers with Roll20?

A: Yes, several free and open-source map makers, such as MapTool, are compatible with Roll20. These tools offer a robust set of features without requiring a subscription.

Q: Do I need to be an artist to create good maps?

A: Not necessarily. Many map makers offer pre-made assets and tools that simplify map creation, even for those without artistic experience. The focus should be on understanding the tools and utilizing them effectively to create visually appealing maps.

Q: What are the best resources for learning how to use map makers?

A: Each map maker typically offers tutorials, online documentation, and community forums where users can share tips and tricks. Additionally, YouTube tutorials and online communities dedicated to map making provide valuable resources for learning and improving skills.

Tips for Creating Effective Maps for Roll20

  • Clarity and Simplicity: Focus on creating maps that are easy to understand and navigate. Avoid overcrowding the map with unnecessary details.
  • Visual Hierarchy: Use color, size, and contrast to highlight important features and guide players’ attention.
  • Scale and Perspective: Consider the scale of the map and the perspective from which players will be viewing it. Ensure that the map’s dimensions are suitable for the virtual tabletop environment.
  • Lighting and Atmosphere: Use lighting and shadows to create a sense of depth and atmosphere. Consider the time of day and weather conditions when designing the map.
  • Tokens and Markers: Choose tokens and markers that are visually distinct and easy to identify. Consider using different colors or symbols to represent different types of entities.

Conclusion

The selection of a map maker for Roll20 is a crucial step in crafting immersive and engaging virtual tabletop experiences. By carefully considering individual needs, preferences, and the strengths and weaknesses of each tool, game masters can choose the best map maker to elevate their campaigns and enhance the visual storytelling potential of their virtual worlds. Whether opting for the ease of use of a web-based platform, the advanced customization options of a desktop application, or the free accessibility of an open-source tool, the right map maker can empower game masters to create visually captivating and memorable gaming experiences.
